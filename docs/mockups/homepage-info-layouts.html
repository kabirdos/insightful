Brief

Five info-layout iterations in the current site style.

Same visual language as insightharness.com (slate + blue + white, Inter, rounded-xl cards with the blue left border). The only thing that changes between iterations is how the new profile data is arranged on the small card, the featured card, and the homepage layout. Real hero/how-it-works/upgrade copy is included so each iteration renders as a real homepage.

Three stages: (1) Five initial iterations (i1–i5). (2) An agent debate over which layout best balances density, scannability, and fit. (3) Five refined iterations (i6–i10) that incorporate the debate findings.

Stage 2 · Debate

5 agents debated the 5 iterations. Here's what they decided.

Five debater roles (Architect, Skeptic, Pragmatist, Innovator, User Champion) ran three rounds of argumentation. Round 1 was independent opening positions. Round 2 was critique and defense. Round 3 was synthesis. A Team Lead (Opus) converged the transcript into a unified plan. Full transcript is in the conversation log.

Decision
Ship a hybrid. Lean small card (identity + hero skill badge + one stat + heatmap). Rich featured card using i2's 3-column stats + workflow chain + heatmaps structure. No LLM signatures, no rule-based personality labels.
The debate converged on a scan/convert split — small cards earn the click, featured cards drive conversion. Crucially, 3 of 5 debaters reversed their positions across rounds: User Champion backed off text signatures after the Pragmatist showed rule-based labels would misfire ~45% of the time. Innovator dropped the text label entirely in favor of a 2× scale skill-badge hero slot. Pragmatist dropped signatures completely.
Survived the debate
  • Scan/convert split — small and featured do different jobs
  • Featured card = i2 — 3-col with workflow chain + heatmaps
  • 7-day heatmap on small card (shape, not data)
  • Hero skill badge at 2× scale on small card (no text)
  • Single curated stat instead of a 2×2 grid
  • 3-column homepage grid preserved (i4 rejected)
Killed in the debate
  • LLM-generated signatures — cost + latency + fragility
  • Rule-based personality labels — ~55% accuracy = landmine
  • i4's 2-column grid — halves browsability
  • i5's density on small cards — visual mud at 320px
  • i2's workflow chain on small cards — "gray blur" when 5/6 cards show same 4 verbs
  • 2×2 stats grid at small size — same four numbers, different cards
Stage 3 directions below. Team Lead identified 5 concrete variants worth building. i6 is the baseline plan. i7–i10 each test a specific tension the debate surfaced but didn't fully resolve (underspecified tag vs skill badge, distinctive stat vs distinctive skill, heatmap as strip vs texture, workflow chain revisited in scan context).

Hero Skill Badge — the debate baseline

A 7-day heatmap strip. Featured card uses i2's 3-column density. Grid stays at 3 columns.
